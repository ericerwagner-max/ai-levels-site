The Levels of AI

From answering a question to running the work itself. The same underlying models power all of these — what changes is how much they see, how much they do, and how much you stay in the loop. Tap any level to expand it.

How to read this: the levels aren't a ranking — higher isn't "better." They're a spectrum of autonomy and integration. Lower levels give you tight control and are perfect for thinking and drafting; higher levels do more of the doing but need clear instructions and review. Most researchers use several at once: chat to brainstorm, an assistant while writing, and Cowork or code when there's a real task to finish. The dots on each card show roughly how much the AI acts on its own.
